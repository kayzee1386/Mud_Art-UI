Mud Art

Mud Art is a dance Company in northern Nigeria, the company was founded by its artistic director and resident choreographer Ochai Ogaba, the creator of mud dance.

Mud Dance

Mud dance is inspired from the substance mud, which is a combination of other elements like earth and water. The emphasis is on the significance of mud in the being of man. The ability to be formed and reformed, to create and serve, even to heal when the man is broken. The dance became a healing place and through these constant expressiveness they started exploring different forms of body movements that turned out to be quiet distinct from other dance styles, it led to the formation of the dance form “mud “.

About

The Company is known for its maiden dance production titled “Mud”. Mud shares a Narrative of the creative process of the mud dance, which came up as a result of a group of dancers northern Nigeria (north western states precisely) who at some stages of their lives suffered from a series of disturbing events like the insurgence of boko haram, sexual abuse and ethno-religious crisis that took the lives of thousands of the people from the region, it led most of them to depression and some mental disorders.

The company has performed in the prestigious Ake festival and Kaduna book and art festival, and it has successfully organized two productions. Mud in 2019 and Plug in 2020 respectively.

Vision

Our vision is to build a dancers in Africa that will tell the stories of its communities through compelling narratives that question religious and political construct that seems to divide the people, to create a safe space to constantly evolve and explore, and ultimately to bring healing to anyone and everyone who needs it.
